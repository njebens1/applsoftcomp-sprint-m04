## Observations

The semantic axes separate companies in intuitive ways. The horizontal axis 
(growth/innovation vs stability/tradition) tends to place technology oriented 
and forward looking companies toward the growth side, while more established 
and traditional companies fall toward the stability side. The vertical axis 
(consumer vs industrial) separates companies based on whether they are consumer 
facing or involved in industrial, manufacturing, or infrastructure activities.

One observation is that companies within the same sector often cluster 
together, which suggests that the embedding model captures similarities in how 
these companies are described in text. However, there are also surprising placements 
where certain companies appear outside their expected regions. This indicates that 
the embeddings are influenced more by language usage and context than strict financial 
or categorical classifications.

A potential third axis could capture company scale or market influence, such as 
small/local versus global/enterprise companies. This would provide additional insight 
into how companies differ beyond innovation and industry focus, helping to better 
separate clusters that currently overlap in the 2D visualization.
